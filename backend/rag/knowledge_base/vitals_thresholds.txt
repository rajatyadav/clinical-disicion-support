CLINICAL VITALS REFERENCE — NORMAL AND ABNORMAL THRESHOLDS

HEART RATE (HR)
- Normal adult: 60–100 bpm
- Bradycardia (low): < 60 bpm — may indicate heart block, hypothyroidism, medication effect
- Tachycardia (high): > 100 bpm — may indicate infection, sepsis, pain, anxiety, dehydration, cardiac arrhythmia
- Critical tachycardia: > 130 bpm — requires immediate evaluation
- Critical bradycardia: < 40 bpm — requires immediate evaluation

BLOOD PRESSURE (BP)
- Normal: Systolic 90–120 mmHg, Diastolic 60–80 mmHg
- Elevated: Systolic 120–129 mmHg
- Stage 1 Hypertension: Systolic 130–139 or Diastolic 80–89 mmHg
- Stage 2 Hypertension: Systolic ≥ 140 or Diastolic ≥ 90 mmHg
- Hypertensive Urgency: Systolic > 180 mmHg without end-organ damage
- Hypertensive Emergency: Systolic > 180 mmHg WITH end-organ damage — requires IV medication
- Hypotension: Systolic < 90 mmHg — can indicate sepsis, hemorrhage, cardiogenic shock
- Mean Arterial Pressure (MAP) goal in sepsis: ≥ 65 mmHg

OXYGEN SATURATION (SpO2)
- Normal: 95–100%
- Mild hypoxemia: 90–94% — supplemental oxygen indicated
- Moderate hypoxemia: 85–89% — escalate oxygen support, consider ABG
- Severe hypoxemia: < 85% — immediate intervention, consider intubation
- COPD patients: acceptable range may be 88–92% due to hypoxic drive

TEMPERATURE
- Normal: 36.1–37.2°C (97–99°F)
- Low-grade fever: 37.3–38.0°C
- Fever: > 38.0°C (> 100.4°F) — infectious workup indicated
- High fever: > 39.0°C — blood cultures, antipyretics, further workup
- Hyperthermia: > 40.0°C — critical, risk of organ damage
- Hypothermia: < 35.0°C — warming measures, cardiac monitoring

RESPIRATORY RATE (RR)
- Normal adult: 12–20 breaths/min
- Tachypnea: > 20 breaths/min — may indicate pain, anxiety, infection, respiratory distress
- Bradypnea: < 12 breaths/min — may indicate opioid overdose, CNS depression
- Critical tachypnea: > 30 breaths/min — respiratory failure risk

COMBINED ABNORMAL PATTERNS
- Sepsis screening (qSOFA): RR > 22, altered mentation, Systolic BP < 100 — 2 or more = high risk
- Shock: HR > 100 + Systolic BP < 90 + altered mentation — emergency
- Respiratory failure: SpO2 < 90% + RR > 25 — consider intubation
- SIRS criteria: Temp > 38°C or < 36°C, HR > 90, RR > 20, WBC abnormal — systemic inflammatory response

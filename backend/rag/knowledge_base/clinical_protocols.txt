CLINICAL PROTOCOLS — SEPSIS, COPD, CARDIAC MONITORING

=== SEPSIS PROTOCOL ===

SEPSIS DEFINITION
Sepsis is life-threatening organ dysfunction caused by a dysregulated host response to infection.
Septic shock: sepsis + vasopressors needed to maintain MAP ≥ 65 + lactate > 2 mmol/L despite fluid resuscitation.

SEPSIS EARLY WARNING SIGNS
- Fever > 38.0°C or hypothermia < 36.0°C
- Heart rate > 90 bpm
- Respiratory rate > 20 bpm or PaCO2 < 32 mmHg
- White blood cell count > 12,000 or < 4,000 cells/μL
- Altered mental status
- Systolic BP < 100 mmHg (qSOFA)

SEPSIS 1-HOUR BUNDLE (Surviving Sepsis Campaign)
1. Measure lactate — re-measure if > 2 mmol/L
2. Obtain blood cultures BEFORE antibiotics
3. Administer broad-spectrum antibiotics within 1 hour
4. Begin IV crystalloid 30 mL/kg for hypotension or lactate ≥ 4 mmol/L
5. Apply vasopressors (norepinephrine first-line) if MAP < 65 mmHg during/after resuscitation

FLAG CRITERIA FOR NURSING ESCALATION
- Any patient with HR > 110 + SpO2 < 93% + Temp > 38.5°C — consider sepsis screening immediately
- Systolic BP drop > 20 mmHg from baseline — notify physician
- Lactate trend upward over 2 readings — escalate

=== COPD EXACERBATION PROTOCOL ===

DEFINITION
Acute worsening of COPD symptoms beyond normal day-to-day variation requiring change in medication.

SEVERITY CLASSIFICATION
- Mild: Increased dyspnea, cough, sputum — treat with short-acting bronchodilators
- Moderate: Significant increase in symptoms — add systemic corticosteroids ± antibiotics
- Severe: Severe dyspnea, cyanosis, use of accessory muscles — may require hospitalization/NIV

OXYGEN TARGETS FOR COPD
- Target SpO2: 88–92% (NOT 94–98% as in normal patients)
- Over-oxygenation can suppress hypoxic drive and cause CO2 retention (hypercapnic respiratory failure)
- Use controlled oxygen therapy — Venturi mask preferred over uncontrolled high-flow

COPD EXACERBATION TREATMENT
1. Short-acting bronchodilators: Albuterol (salbutamol) + Ipratropium nebulized q4h
2. Corticosteroids: Prednisone 40mg PO daily x 5 days
3. Antibiotics (if purulent sputum or fever): Azithromycin or Doxycycline
4. Non-invasive ventilation (NIV/BiPAP): if pH < 7.35 + PaCO2 > 45
5. Intubation if NIV fails or contraindicated

NURSING ESCALATION TRIGGERS FOR COPD
- SpO2 < 85% despite supplemental O2
- Respiratory rate > 30 bpm
- Altered mental status / somnolence
- Inability to speak full sentences

=== CARDIAC MONITORING PROTOCOL ===

POST-MI MONITORING
After acute myocardial infarction, continuous cardiac monitoring is required for minimum 24–48h.

MONITORING PARAMETERS
- Continuous 12-lead or telemetry monitoring
- BP every 1–2 hours
- 12-lead ECG if symptoms recur
- Daily troponin if indicated
- Watch for: new ST changes, arrhythmias, BP instability

HYPERTENSIVE EMERGENCY PROTOCOL
- Definition: Systolic > 180 OR Diastolic > 120 WITH end-organ damage
- End-organ damage signs: chest pain, dyspnea, AMS, visual changes, neurological deficits
- Treatment: IV labetalol, nicardipine, or sodium nitroprusside
- Goal: Reduce MAP by 25% in first hour (NOT normalize immediately — risk of stroke/MI)
- AVOID rapid BP lowering — can precipitate ischemia in chronically hypertensive patients

CARDIAC ALERT CRITERIA
- New ST elevation > 1mm in 2 contiguous leads — activate STEMI protocol
- Sustained HR > 150 or < 40 — immediate evaluation
- Systolic BP < 80 mmHg in cardiac patient — notify physician immediately
- New onset chest pain with diaphoresis — 12-lead ECG within 10 minutes

=== GENERAL NURSING ESCALATION PROTOCOL ===

WHEN TO CALL RAPID RESPONSE TEAM
- Acute change in HR: > 130 or < 40 bpm
- Acute change in BP: Systolic < 80 or > 200 mmHg
- SpO2 < 88% despite oxygen
- Respiratory rate > 30 or < 8 bpm
- Acute change in mental status
- Nurse clinical concern — trust your instinct

MODIFIED EARLY WARNING SCORE (MEWS)
Score 0–1 per vital sign parameter; total score interpretation:
- 0–1: Routine monitoring
- 2–3: Increase monitoring frequency, notify charge nurse
- 4–5: Notify physician, consider ICU transfer
- ≥ 6: Consider immediate physician evaluation / rapid response
